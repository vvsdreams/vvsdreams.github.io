Since my childhood, I've always felt some degree of wonderment towards computing. Watching as capacitors encapsulated the world as I knew it and inconvenience becoming a thing of the past.

My interest began in programming spawned after learning how to build a computer, I wanted the computer to learn how to work for me. So I embarked on this journey of teaching myself how to program at the age of 14; I decided that C++ was the language I should learn... Needless to say this interest was short-lived and I went back to playing League of Legends.

Years of my life passed and interests came and went along the way. Late 2022 I began to feel the pains of comfortability, I felt as if everything I was doing wasn't challenging me in a fulfilling way. A friend of mine had been showing me the programming work he was doing in college, and it began to re-kindle that interest of developing software. I messed with Python a little here and there to build social media automation tools, but they were API derived, I didn't feel fully satisfied because it wasn't entirely my creation. I decided that front-end development would be my entry point into this new world.

This year, getting into the Udacity program certainly motivated me to actually open my IDE and code. I have watched tons of video on CS theory and how to code certain things, but I always felt this hesitation to open the IDE and actually try it. Leading up to acceptance in the front-end development program, I practiced the FreeCodeCamp HTML course and became somewhat proficient. This is where I am at now and this website is currently a testament to the world I have put in, whether it's impressive or not is up for you to decide.
